A Game I Made

Stackimals

Once every quarter Funsize shuts down for a week for a company event known as Method. I joined Funsize in February, so Method week #12 was my first one and I was SO STOKED to get to participate. The week's theme was "Create" and we were given the freedom to create whatever we wanted. We all documented our process and then virtually got together on Friday to share-out our work. It was an incredible experience. I feel so lucky and grateful to be working for an agency that supports this level of work-life balance and team culture.

Ever since creating my augmented reality art card I've wanted to do more experiments with AR. So for one of my projects I decided to make a game. Here's a screenshot of my idea as it was captured in my sketchbook.

In addition to creating Stackimals I was also working on an illustration and a sound visualization experiment. It was a tall order to get the game to a playable state because I had to learn how to use SceneKit, design and model the 3D assets, create sound effects, come up with a visual style for the UI, produce assets, and write game logic.

I didn't accomplish everything I wanted, so right now it's not much of a "game." But I have plans to incrementally update it over time to make the game more fun and engaging. It's too basic to be submitted to the AppStore, but if you are interested in playing with the app or looking at the code then hmu, I'm happy to share!
